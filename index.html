About Me

My name is Roberto Hernandez. I'm originally from Mexico City but am currently living here in Querétaro. By profession, I am a Data Analyst for a global nutrition company, where I enjoy diving into complex datasets to uncover business insights. A good example of my work is a recent project where I developed a call volume forecast for a large call centre. This involved analysing a detailed, three-year dataset—broken down into 30-minute intervals across multiple languages and agent skills—to accurately predict future trends. I primarily work within the Microsoft ecosystem to build practical, enterprise-level solutions. Outside of my professional life, I like to maintain a healthy balance. I'm an avid runner, I enjoy exploring my creative side through cooking, and I always have some music on in the background.

Querétaro, México

Santiago de Querétaro is a city in central Mexico. It’s known for its well-preserved Spanish colonial architecture such as a striking pink stone aqueduct. Notable baroque buildings include Santa Rosa de Viterbo church. The nearby ochre San Francisco church is in Zenea Garden, the city’s main square. In the old church convent, the Querétaro Regional Museum displays pre-Hispanic, colonial and republican artifacts.
